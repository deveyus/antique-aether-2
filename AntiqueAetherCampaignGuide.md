### The Setting: The Witchurin System

For more than a century, the Witchurin system was a ghost town—a dead industrial graveyard bypassed by history. To the Coreward side sits the Barlegut System, locked in a grinding, three-way civil war. To the Rimward side lies Vorgen, a lawless buffer state of warlords and scavengers hemmed in by massive, rival superpower empires.

Between them lay nothing but an empty, brutal Jump-3 void. Nobody built here. Nobody claimed it.

Until now.

### The Jewel in the Dark

Seemingly overnight, the AAA megacorp Zyarth Orbital (ZO) has dropped a pristine, multi-billion-credit Class B Starport right over the swirling purple atmosphere of the gas giant Witchurin-2. To the galaxy, it is a gleaming triumph of corporate engineering and a bold free-port initiative to open trade routes where none existed.

But anyone with a spacer's eye can feel the uncanny weight of the place. The gleaming towers, the automated repair yards, the polished docks—it is a massive declaration of intent. But why here? Why now? Why spend the GDP of an entire star system to anchor a luxury station on the absolute edge of the map?

### The Player’s Reality: A Week in the Dark

In this universe, space travel is governed by ironclad laws: every jump takes exactly one week in the blind void, no matter the distance. Out here at a Jump-3 nexus, you are fundamentally isolated. Help, reinforcements, and news from the Core don't arrive via tight-beam; they take months of carefully staged transit to reach the rim.

To the casual traveler or visiting merchant, Zyarth Station appears fully operational, stable, and firmly under corporate control. But behind the heavy bulkheads, in the quiet executive offices, there is a distinct sense of isolation. The corporate bureaucracy is moving with a strange, deliberate friction. Highly specific, sensitive problems are appearing on the promenade and across the system's dead moons.

### The Influx

You aren't the only ones who noticed the gold rush.

*   **Comunex Integrated:** A rapacious, AAA consumer goods and data leviathan from the Core is already moving onto the station promenade, deploying high-end tech suites to aggressively dominate the local markets.
*   **The Shipping Lines:** Lumbering bulk freighters from Barony Bulk Freight and elite, high-risk blockade runners from the Meridian Void-Guild are choking the docking queues, bringing in resources from the warring balkanized systems nearby.

### Your Role

You are freelancers, specialists, independent spacer crews, or unvouched-for assets who just made the brutal trek to the rim. Whether you are fleeing a corporate contract back in the Core, looking to salvage pre-Collapse tech from the radioactive ruins of Witchurin-1, or hunting for high-paying security work, you have arrived at the perfect time.

Zyarth Orbital’s local management is handing out lucrative, off-the-books contracts for "specialized consulting." They need people who don't wear a corporate uniform, people who can operate in the gray spaces where official security cannot tread, and above all, people who know how to keep their mouths shut.

The station has just opened its doors. The air is thick with unspoken tension, corporate paranoia, and the eerie quiet of a frontier waiting to fracture. Welcome to Witchurin.

### Game Features

*   **Hard Sci-Fi Logistics:** Travel, fuel management, and specialized gear matter. Crossing a Jump-3 void requires elite ships, drop-tanks, or extreme preparation.
*   **System Sandbox:** Explore a completely mapped system, from the high-tech corporate intrigue of the Zyarth Promenade to the tidally locked, radioactive ruins of Witchurin-1 and the frozen, abandoned colony moons of Witchurin-3.
*   **Faction Noir:** Navigate a web of desperate civil war factions, predatory megacorps, and lawless rim scavengers where your reputation is your only real currency.
